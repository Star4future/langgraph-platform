### Q: What is the AMC (Australian Mathematics Competition)?

### Q: What is the "6-dimension feedback rubric" I keep seeing?
**Short answer:** It's the scoring system we use for writing — marking each essay on Ideas, Structure, Vocabulary, Fluency, Voice, and Conventions — the same six criteria used in selective exam marking.

**Detail:**
- **Ideas:** Is the argument or story idea strong, original, and relevant to the prompt?
- **Structure:** Does the piece have a clear beginning, middle, and end? Are paragraphs logical?
- **Vocabulary:** Is word choice precise, varied, and appropriate?
- **Fluency:** Does the writing flow naturally? Are sentences varied in length and rhythm?
- **Voice:** Does the writing have a distinctive, consistent tone?
- **Conventions:** Grammar, spelling, punctuation — markers do penalise errors here
- Each dimension is scored individually so students know exactly where to improve

## CATEGORY F: How It Actually Works

### Q: Is this live tutoring or self-study?
**Short answer:** Ace Achievers is a structured self-study platform — your child works at their own pace, guided by AI. It is not live tutoring with a human teacher in real time.

**Detail:**
- No scheduled sessions, no Zoom calls — your child works on their own schedule
- The AI provides feedback within seconds of submitting work — faster than waiting for a tutor
- Structured weekly plans keep students on track without needing a human to chase them
- Human expert reviews are available on higher tiers (M1 Full Prep, M2 Mentored) for one-on-one written feedback
- Many families use Ace Achievers alongside a school tutor — the platform does the repetition and feedback; the tutor does the strategic coaching

---

### Q: How much time does my child need to spend each week?
**Short answer:** We recommend 30 minutes a day, 5 days a week. That's 2.5 hours per week — enough to make consistent progress without burnout.

**Detail:**
- Maths/Science: 20-30 minutes of structured problem practice per session
- Writing: one essay draft per week (about 45-60 minutes) is the rhythm that drives improvement
- Students can do more — the platform is self-paced and unlimited on monthly/annual plans
- Progress reports sent every Sunday summarise exactly what was covered and what to focus on next

---

### Q: What does a typical week look like on the platform?
**Short answer:** Students follow a weekly lesson plan — topic lesson → practice problems → AI feedback → optional mock → next topic. The cycle is the same across maths, science, and writing.

**Detail:**
- **Monday/Tuesday:** Core lesson (concept explanation + worked examples)
- **Wednesday/Thursday:** Practice problems at graduated difficulty
- **Friday:** Mini mock or AI-graded essay submission
- **Sunday:** Parent progress report delivered by email
- Students can move ahead if they're fast, or repeat a section if they need more practice

---

### Q: Can I as a parent track my child's progress?
**Short answer:** Yes — a parent progress report is emailed every Sunday, covering topics mastered, score trend, and recommended focus for the coming week.

**Detail:**
- Weekly email report: topics covered · score trend · where to focus · time spent
- Dashboard access (Monthly and above): log in anytime to see your child's problem history, scores, and streak
- School plan: teacher dashboard with cohort-level analytics included

---

### Q: What if my child gets completely stuck on a problem?
**Short answer:** The three-tier hint system is designed for exactly this — first a nudge, then a fuller guide, then a complete worked solution. No student should hit a wall with no way forward.

**Detail:**
- Tier 1 Nudge: a question or clue that points the student toward the right approach — no answer given
- Tier 2 Guide: a partial worked solution — shows the first key step
- Tier 3 Solution: complete step-by-step worked solution with explanations
- Students earn more from trying Tier 1 first — but Tier 3 is always available so nobody gets stuck for hours
- Writing equivalent: after submitting a draft, the AI gives specific sentence-level suggestions, not just a score
